Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2025 · This policy explains how DataLens collects, uses, and protects your information when you visit this website.

◈ Information we collect

▼

DataLens is a static informational website. We do not have user accounts, login forms, or collect any personally identifiable information directly. The following data may be collected automatically:

  • Usage data — pages visited, time spent, tabs clicked, and navigation patterns via Google Analytics 4.
  • Device data — browser type, operating system, screen resolution, and device category (desktop/mobile).
  • Location data — approximate country and city inferred from your IP address (not stored by us directly).
  • Referral data — the website or search engine that referred you to DataLens.

◎ How we use your information

▼

The data collected is used solely to understand how visitors interact with the website and to improve its content and structure. Specifically:

  • To see which industry tabs, blog posts, and sections are most popular.
  • To understand where our visitors come from and what devices they use.
  • To improve content quality, navigation, and site performance over time.
  • To measure the overall reach and growth of the website.

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling, remarketing, or sell it to any third parties.

◇ Google Analytics

▼

This website uses Google Analytics 4 (GA4) to collect anonymous usage statistics. GA4 uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to measure user interactions.

  • Google may process your data on servers located outside your country.
  • GA4 data is anonymized and aggregated — we cannot identify individual users.
  • Google's data processing is governed by Google's Privacy Policy.
  • You can opt out via the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

◉ Cookies

▼

DataLens itself does not set any first-party cookies. Google Analytics sets the following cookies on your device:

  • _ga — distinguishes unique users. Expires after 2 years.
  • _ga_XXXXXXX — maintains session state. Expires after 2 years.
  • _gid — distinguishes users. Expires after 24 hours.

You can manage or delete cookies through your browser settings at any time. Disabling cookies may affect how some parts of the site function.

◫ Data retention

▼

Analytics data is retained in Google Analytics for 14 months by default, after which it is automatically deleted. We do not store any analytics data on our own servers. Since DataLens is a static website hosted on GitHub Pages, no user data is stored in any database by us directly.

◑ Third-party links

▼

DataLens may contain links to external websites including LinkedIn, GitHub, and other resources. We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of those external sites. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party sites you visit.

◁ Your rights

▼

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights regarding your data:

  • Right to access — request information about what data is collected about you.
  • Right to deletion — request that your data be deleted.
  • Right to opt-out — opt out of analytics tracking using the Google opt-out tool.
  • GDPR (EU users) — rights including access, rectification, and erasure under GDPR.
  • CCPA (California users) — right to know what personal information is collected and to request deletion.

Since we collect no personally identifiable information directly, most data-related requests should be directed to Google Analytics.

◔ Changes to this policy

▼

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for legal reasons. When we do, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of the website after changes are posted constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.
